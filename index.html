Nigeria, Abuja

Nigeria, often called the “Giant of Africa,” is a West African country with a large population and a diverse culture of over 250 ethnic groups, with English as the official language. Rich in natural resources like oil, Nigeria has vibrant cities, music, and traditions. Its capital, Abuja, located in the center of the country, was purpose-built in the 1980s to replace Lagos and is known for its modern layout and landmarks such as Aso Rock, the National Mosque, and the National Church, serving as the political and administrative hub of the nation.
